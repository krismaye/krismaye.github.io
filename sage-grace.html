Sage & Grace

About the Client

Sage & Grace is a website and concierge service that educates and empowers grieving families to better, and more affordably, navigate the complications and stress of the American funeral planning process. Many consumers start their funeral planning process by going online, but the disparity and inferiority of the information available online results in misinformation, confusion, and stress. By using the Sage & Grace online platform, which clearly organizes information and tailors funeral planning options based on consumer needs, consumers will be able to make informed decisions throughout the process.

User Research

Our user research included 6 user interviews and a survey with 60+ respondents. After conducting our interviews, we created an affinity wall in order to better understand the current complexities and pain points in the funeral planning process. Based on these findings, we found that those who had planned funerals before were less likely to turn to the internet, instead choosing to use their prior experiences or recommendations from friends or family when choosing a funeral home. This led us to narrow down our target demographic to novice funeral planners.

Our interviews and surveys also indicated to us that one of the main concerns of novice funeral planners is cost. The average American funeral costs $10,000, so it's no surprise that consumers would want to keep costs down where possible. This finding illustrated that we needed to make pricing information a main feature of the website.

After compiling all of the findings from our user research, we created a primary persona, Jenny: a woman with no funeral planning experience who wants to plan a funeral to honor her mother, on a budget. We storyboarded Jenny's current funeral planning process so we could identify areas where Sage & Grace could improve this process for her.

Updated Project Goal

Find a trustworthy and simple way to help novices
conquer the complexity in finding a suitable funeral home

Prototypes

Based on the results of our research, we wanted to incorporate several things into our design. First, in order to help build trust with users, we devoted most of the homepage to a video - allowing space to showcase what Sage & Grace is, and how it can support users through their funeral planning process. We also wanted to make searching for funeral homes quick and easy, so we placed the search feature front and center on the homepage as well. Further down on the page we also included further information about what Sage & Grace provides for users, as well as featured stories from users - another trust-building feature.

Our search page contained a wide variety of filters to allow users to narrow down the options to those that would best meet their needs. We also chose to include average prices, since our research showed that funeral costs were a big concern during the funeral planning process. From the search page, users can also choose multiple funeral homes to compare, allowing them to further look at the differences in price and offering at each home. As a final trust-building feature, we also included a photo and biography of the funeral director on the page for each individual funeral home, to allow users to become familiar with who they will be working with.

Ultimately, our final digital prototype illustrated the process of searching for, and comparing, funeral homes. Our chosen design provides users with the tools they need to begin to compare funeral homes, and empowering them with the information they need for the next steps of their planning process.
