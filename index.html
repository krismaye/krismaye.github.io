SI 582: Introduction to Interaction Design

SI 582: Introduction to Interaction Design

September 2016 - December 2016

  • Problem statement

    As an incoming graduate student at the University of Michigan, it can be difficult to find housing on or around campus. Because many graduate students are coming from places outside the state of Michigan, or even outside the United States, they may not be able come to Ann Arbor to visit potential apartments, and may have to conduct their housing search from afar. This can make it more difficult to find a suitable living arrangement. Additionally, graduate students may be interested in sharing housing with one or more roommates to cut down on costs, but may not know any other students with whom they could live, especially if they are incoming first year students. Even for returning students who may be looking to move to a different apartment, the process can be difficult because there is not necessarily one centralized place to look for apartment listings. These factors can make the housing search stressful for graduate students. I would like to design a technical intervention to help the University of Michigan graduate student population more easily find housing and/or roommates in Ann Arbor.

  • Workflows of possible solutions make searching for apartments and roommates easier for graduate students

  • Proposed Solution

    Create an app that combines a consolidated, filterable map of housing listings in Ann Arbor with a roommate matching system to help students find somewhere to live and someone to live with.

    Peer Feedback

    After feedback from several peers, I decided to differentiate my app by making the primary focus searching for and matching with compatible roommates rather than finding an apartment.

Real Time Farms Usability Researcher

Real Time Farms Usability Researcher

January 2012 - April 2012

Real Time Farms is devoted to the collaborative efforts between restaurants, farmers markets, farmers, and consumers to post information about food and ingredients online. The Real Time Farms website consists of 3 major pages: food, farmers markets, and eateries. Each of these options can be accessed via a menu bar at the top of the site or the map interface on the home page. Within these pages, visitors to the site can find food, farms, markets, and restaurants located anywhere in the world, though a majority of establishments are located in the United States. The website also includes features that enable site visitors to search for individual farms, markets and restaurants, and which ingredients these establishments use and where they have come from.

As the final project for SI 422: Evaluation of Systems and Services, I, along with a team of four others, created a report on the Real Time Farms website that looked at the usability of the site. Our report included our own thoughts, as well as findings obtained through interviews and testing with others who were unfamiliar with the site.
